Explanatory Data Analysis of Companies Bankrupcy Risk

Introduction

1. Business Context

Our client is a Central European investment firm.

They have a large financial dataset that contains important indicators of the risk of a company going bankrupt. Among other important financial metrics, this dataset includes debt ratio percentage, borrowing dependency, liability-to-equity ratio, and interest coverage ratio.

The client intends to use this information to improve their risk assessment techniques and investment decision-making process when evaluating potential companies for investment.

It is my role as a data science consultant to do an Explanatory Data Analysis (EDA) in order to find notable patterns and relevant details in the dataset. This will help the company optimise its investment approach and make good data-driven decisions to minimise risks by looking at the risk indicators linked to bankruptcy.

2. Objectives

  1. Clean and analyze the data to meet clients demand by answering the business questions.

  2. EDA is performed in order to provide actionable insights which will be further used cleaning and processing the data, that will help the investment firm enhance its risk assessment strategies.

3. Data Source

This data set is pulled from a public source of Kaggle.com. The link to the data set is:

https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/fedesoriano/company-bankruptcy-prediction?select=data.csv

4. Business Questions

  1. Which financial ratios are the best indicators of bankruptcy?

  2. If we want to avoid bankruptcy, what are the early red signs?

  3. How does a company's debt load affect how likely it is to become bankrupt?

  4. When evaluating a company's danger of bankruptcy, which financial indicators should investors focus on?

  5. if we want to take a frim out from bankruptcy which features should be restored back and focused on?

The matrix gives a broader view of the correlation between the features.

High-positive corelation:

Higher Net Value Per Share (B) indicates companies financial stability which gives higher Persistent EPS in the Last Four Seasons.

Borrowing depency is directly related to Current Liabilities/Equity which says, companies with lower borrowing depency will have lower liability/equity and hence wont go bankrupt

Companies with higher Retained Earnings to Total Assets also have higher ROA(C) before interest and depreciation. Persistent EPS in the Last Four Seasons is also directly proportional to ROA(C) before interest and depreciation.

Higher working capital positively increases net income of the company.

High-Negative corelation:

Companies with higher borrowing depency have lower working capital and lower net income to stockholder's equilty. Similarly, companies with higher liability/equity has lower working capital and lower net income to stockholder's equilty.

Companies with higher equity-to-liability ratio has lower debt ratio. That shows they have more equity relative to liabilities.

Moderate corelation:

Low debt ratio indicates company might also have a low borrowing dependency. Higher retained earnings indicates higher EPS as earnings can be used to reinvest within the company resulting in increased EPS.

features = [" Debt ratio %"," ROA(C) before interest and depreciation before interest", " Net Income to Stockholder's Equity"," Current Liabilities/Equity"]

df_bankrupt, df_healthy = df[df["Bankrupt?"] == 1], df[df["Bankrupt?"] == 0]

fig, axes = plt.subplots(2, 2, figsize=(14, 6))
for i, feature in enumerate(features):
    row = i // 2
    col = i % 2
    ax = axes[row, col]
    sns.kdeplot(df_bankrupt[feature], label="Bankrupt", fill=True, color="orange", alpha=0.75, ax=ax)
    sns.kdeplot(df_healthy[feature], label="Healthy", fill=True, color="red", alpha=0.5, ax=ax)
    ax.set_title(f"Distribution of {feature} (Bankrupt vs. Healthy)")
    ax.legend()

plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
